Learn about the 5 types of autonomous driving.

We review the five levels of driving that exist according to SAE regulations and the cars that are currently marketed in our market.

Level 1 autonomous driving

The vast majority of cars currently sold already have one or more level 1 autonomous driving systems where the driver continues to play the leading role in driving, but has technology that helps him during his journeys. Examples would be adaptive cruise control, emergency braking, or lane change assistant, which not only warns but also makes a slight gesture over direction.

Level 2 autonomous driving

This group is the one that is growing the most in recent times and, as you will see, current cars are high-end. The first ones that usually receive the latest technology. At level 2 cars can already act independently on certain occasions. As examples we have the Drive Pilot that equips the Mercedes, the Autopilot of Tesla, the “traffic jam assist” of the Audi or the driving system in traffic jams of the Volvo.Although with the Autopilot system from Tesla we should make a small note. Due to the existing regulations in Spain, its system is required for the driver to touch the steering wheel at all times, while as seen in many YouTube videos, in the United States many people circulate without touching it. In summary, it would be a level 3 technology, but due to the legislation it is still 2.

Level 3 autonomous driving

Cars belonging to level 3 may perform autonomous functions as long as certain requirements are met. For example, the new Audi A8 has a system called "traffic jam pilot" in which they can drive only on multi-lane roads with central separation and up to a speed of 80 km / h.

Level 4 autonomous driving

Goodbye driver. It is true that it will be in certain situations adapted for autonomous driving, but at level 4 the car will already be able to perform certain functions without having to have a driver on board. These will be circulating on highways overtaking, automatic parking in parking where it will only be enough to leave the car at the door or even some taxis in limited areas of the cities. At the moment there is no car on the market, but many in development.

Level 5 autonomous driving

The car will go from being a personal entity to a simple vehicle that takes you to places. They will no longer need a steering wheel, you will mount in the car and it will take you to the destination that you indicate in a completely autonomous way. This could be the end of the physical taxi driver as such. Since the brands are almost developing level 4, there are no cars yet, marking as possible dates for their sale from 2030.
