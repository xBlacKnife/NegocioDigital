The World’s Top 5 (or so) Luxury Motorcycles

What image do motorcycles conjure? Bad boys in denim and black leather roaring through sleepy towns, scattering dogs, grannies and terrified children in their wake? Serious looking Germans and Scandinavians crossing continents with luggage piled high? Or the sublime blend of courage, supreme skill and cutting edge technology that enables the world’s best riders to lap the Isle of Man’s TT Mountain Course at an average speed of over 133 mph?. Here are some my favourite luxury motorbikes … some icons, some clichés and, I hope, one or two surprises.

The Honda Goldwing – The epitome of luxury motorcycling

It all began as a 1000cc naked roadster in 1975 and evolved in capacity and opulence over the years to an 1100, 1200, 1500cc luxury tourer. We are now on the second generation of 6-cy 1800cc intercontinental mile-burners. All-day comfort, electronic rider aids, on-bike information and entertainment systems, copious luggage capacity and, for the convenience of riders and the amazement of onlookers, a reverse gear. Another Honda snapping at the heels of the mighty Goldwing is the ST1300 Pan European. It’s almost as accomplished a long distance mile-muncher and it’s smaller, more agile, more sporty. In my opinion, it’s easier to live with as a day-to-day ride and probably the one I would prefer. Having said that, I once followed a Miami policeman on a Honda Goldwing down a canyon near Seattle and he was throwing it about like a sports bike.

The American dream – Harley Davidson’s Road Glide Ultra

One big tourer I couldn’t leave out because it completely epitomises the open-road motorcycling lifestyle – and the American dream – is Harley Davidson’s biggest, most extravagant long distance cruiser: the Road Glide Ultra. Massive torque from its new Milwaukee-Eight 107 engine, new front and rear suspension for improved handling and all the latest touring technology like ABS brakes and sophisticated infotainment that is expected in this class of motorcycle. I’ve ridden Harleys in their natural habitat, the American highway, and I adore them. They are not fast, they are heavy and they don’t handle particularly well but, if motorcycles could have a soul, then it would burn most brightly in a “Hog”.

The Suzuki GSX-R750 – Thank you, Japanese engineering

The Japanese make phenomenal sports bikes, too. It’s a huge achievement that for the price of a used car you can walk into a motorcycle showroom and ride away on a Honda, Yamaha, Suzuki or Kawasaki that is beautifully engineered, easy to ride and just a whisker slower than a full-race bike. Choosing between them is a matter of taste and personal loyalty.My favourite is the Suzuki GSX-R750. For me, it’s the perfect compromise in size and power… and it’s gorgeous in blue and white

A triumphant return – The classic Bonneville

Probably the best known of the retro bikes is the Triumph Bonneville. A thoroughly modern motorcycle introduced in 2001, it takes almost all its styling cues from the iconic T120 Bonneville of the 1960s and is hard to distinguish from it at a distance. The Bonneville is the ideal steed for those who want to recapture the spirit of the 50s and 60s but on a modern, safe and reliable motorcycle. A real classic bike, like a T120, is great fun but it’s oily, unreliable, difficult to start and expensive to buy and own. This Bonneville is just as much fun but without the hassle. Excellent!

The Harley Road King – The ultimate bad boy bikes

Many of the original choppers were based on Harley Davidsons and Harley has always been very good at understanding and supporting this segment of the market. Many have copied them and there are many better bikes. But for me, in the bad boy cruiser class, it has to be a Harley, it’s a motorcycling icon. I’ll have a Road King in deep blue with as many Screamin’ Eagle accessories as I can afford. Oh, and a matt black open face helmet and reflector shades. Black leather jacket, denims, riggers boots. Sunshine and the open road… I’m drifting off there now. And an ice-cold beer at the end of the ride… doesn’t get better than that!
